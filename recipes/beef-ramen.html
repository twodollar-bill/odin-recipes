One-Pot Beef Ramen Noodles

Photo by Luis Becerra Fotógrafo: https://www.pexels.com/photo/close-up-shot-of-delicious-ramen-in-ceramic-bowl-5774085/

Description

This one-pot beef ramen hits your table in as little as 30 minutes. It is packed with flavor and comfort for those cold winter or rainy nights. All you need is ground beef, veggies, ramen noodles, and a broth!

Ingredients (4 servings)

  • 1 lb 93/7 ground beef
  • 2 packets ramen noodles
  • 3 cups thinly sliced cabbage
  • 1 1/2 cups thinly sliced red bell pepper
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 cup matchstick carrots
  • Thinly sliced scallions
  • Toasted sesame seeds

Directions

  1. Gather all the ingredients.
  2. Heat a medium dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook, stirring occasionally to break the meat into crumbles. Cook until browned and no longer pink, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in vegetables. Cook until vegetables begin to wilt and cabbage turns translucent, about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in broth, soy sauce, and hoisin sauce. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Once at a boil, add ramen, spooning the beef mixture over the ramen as needed in order to fully submerge the ramen. Reduce heat to medium. Cover and cook undisturbed for 5 to 7 minutes or until ramen is tender. Stir in carrots, cover and remove from heat, and let sit for 1 to 2 minutes or until carrots are tender.
  5. Garnish with scallions and sesame seeds. Serve.
